House 28 is a multi-apartment building located at the boundary between dense urban development and the suburbs. The main architectural concept is a smooth transition from suburban to urban style, expressed through a gradient of materials and forms.

The left side of the building offers apartments with spacious terraces and mini-plots, creating a cozy atmosphere and a connection to nature. As you move toward the right, the terraces are replaced by French balconies, and the façade becomes more rigid and monumental, reflecting the urban environment.

The building combines concrete and wood, where concrete reflects the urban rhythm, while wood, stained to resemble walnut, emphasizes a more suburban character. This combination of materials creates a visual dialogue between urban and suburban environments, forming a new type of housing that harmoniously bridges two different worlds.
