BUNKER is a residential module surrounded by a protective shell, designed to survive harsh radioactive contamination conditions. Inside, there's everything needed for living for 30 years. In the absence of radioactive contamination, the Bunker operates as a regular residential module.

The project focuses on creating a living space inspired by personal needs and fears, especially the fear of ionizing radiation. The main idea of the project is safety. For this purpose, a residential module with an area of 28 m² was created, which is a bunker located in the center of Iceland, as a place where the risk of nuclear events is minimal.

The work draws attention to the issue of survival after the apocalypse and the precarious state of our world, highlighting the enormous costs of saving a small group of people and the irreversibility of the consequences of nuclear war.

MODULE is a living space located in the center of the bunker, with an area of 28m2, and consists of a bathroom and a living room. The space is designed for four users.

Zoning of the space allows combining a work area, a rest area, and a kitchen in one room, satisfying all the necessary needs of the residents. Special attention is paid to psychological comfort in conditions of prolonged isolation - there is an electronic library, a workshop, and a farm.

Lighting, which imitates the day cycle, helps create the illusion of being outdoors. Concrete slabs form sections for storage.

Outside the module, the bunker represents a technical area, where all the defense and life support systems are located, as well as storage areas. Resources are calculated for 30 years of isolation from the outside world. The axonometry shows the internal structure of the bunker, systems, and module are highlighted in red.

The bunker uses a hydrogenerator as a source of energy, located in the energy zone. The difference in heights and a local waterfall allows to generate large volumes of electricity without harm to the environment.

The shape of the bunker facade is inspired by the sand-sunken rocks of Iceland. Inspired by their beauty and abundance of details, I decided to simplify the shape of the rock, using the method of triangulating polygons - which allowed creating a unique protective shell capable of withstanding the most powerful loads.
